HANGZHOU CUISINE

A Culinary Paradise

HANGZHOU FOOD CULTURE

Hangzhou cuisine, also known as Lu Cuisine, is one of the Eight Major Cuisines of China. Known for its delicate flavors, elegant presentation, and emphasis on fresh ingredients, Hangzhou cuisine reflects the refined culture of this beautiful lakeside city. From imperial dishes to street snacks, every bite tells a story of Hangzhou's thousand-year heritage.

CLASSIC DISHES

These signature dishes have been perfected over centuries and remain beloved by locals and visitors alike.

Dongpo Pork

BRAISED PORK BELLY

Braised pork belly slow-cooked with soy sauce, rice wine, and aromatics for over 4 hours. This legendary dish was created in honor of the poet Su Dongpo. The meat becomes incredibly tender, melting in your mouth while maintaining its shape. Each piece is served in a small clay pot, glistening with rich, caramelized sauce.

Dragon Well Shrimp

TEA-INFUSED SHRIMP

Fresh river shrimp delicately stir-fried with Longjing tea leaves, creating a perfect harmony of land and water. The dish captures the essence of Hangzhou's famous tea culture. The shrimp are plump and sweet, infused with the subtle fragrance of premium dragon well green tea, making it a true delicacy.

West Lake Fish

SWEET AND SOUR FISH

Fresh mandarin fish from West Lake cooked to perfection and served with a sweet-sour sauce made from aged vinegar. This dish originated from the Song Dynasty and represents the essence of Hangzhou cuisine. The fish is incredibly tender with a delicate briny flavor, perfectly balanced by the tangy glaze.

Beggar's Chicken

MUD-ROASTED CHICKEN

A whole chicken wrapped in lotus leaves and mud, then slow-roasted for hours until the meat becomes fall-off-the-bone tender. This legendary dish dates back to the Qing Dynasty. The clay coating seals in all the juices and aromas, resulting in incredibly flavorful and aromatic meat.

Sliced Noodles

PIAN ER CHUAN NOODLES

A beloved Hangzhou street food featuring hand-cut noodles in a rich pork bone broth with pickled vegetables, bamboo shoots, and lean pork. This warming dish has been a staple of Hangzhou's breakfast culture for over a century. The sour-accented broth perfectly complements the chewy noodles.

Xiaolongbao

STEAMED SOUP DUMPLINGS

Delicate steamed buns filled with pork and savory aspic that creates a burst of rich, hot soup when you bite into them. Though originally from nearby Shanghai, Hangzhou has adopted this dumpling with its own local variations. The thin, translucent skin holds precious soup within.

DINING RECOMMENDATIONS

  • Louwailou Restaurant - Founded in 1848, this legendary restaurant on the shores of West Lake serves authentic Hangzhou cuisine with dishes like West Lake Fish in Vinegar Sauce and Dragon Well Shrimp.
  • Hubin Road - This bustling food street offers a mix of traditional Hangzhou dishes and modern culinary experiences, perfect for evening strolls and dinner.
  • Gao Street - Known as Hangzhou's Food Street, featuring over 60 restaurants offering cuisines from across China and beyond.
  • Wulin Night Market - Experience local street food culture with vendors selling late into the night, including grilled skewers, stinky tofu, and sweet rice balls.
  • Tea Houses - Many traditional tea houses serve light meals and snacks alongside Longjing tea, offering an authentic Hangzhou cultural experience.
