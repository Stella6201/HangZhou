LINGYIN TEMPLE

Founded in 326 AD, Lingyin Temple is one of the oldest and most significant Buddhist temples in China. Nestled in the misty valley of Flying Peak, this ancient monastery has witnessed the rise and fall of dynasties while preserving its spiritual heritage for over 1,700 years.

The temple complex features magnificent halls adorned with intricate carvings and ancient Buddhist statues. The Hall of Five Hundred Arhats houses 500 bronze figures, each with unique expressions and poses that showcase extraordinary craftsmanship from the Ming Dynasty.

Beyond the main temple, the surrounding Feilai Feng valley contains over 300 rock carvings dating from the 10th to 14th centuries. These Buddhist images, carved directly into the limestone cliffs, represent one of the finest collections of ancient Buddhist art in China.
